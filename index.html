ORIGIN STORY

Once, Europe ran on fire — coal, oil, gas — pulled from deep places and sent up the chimney. The lights came on. The sky paid the bill.

Then a generation of heroes rose from the rooftops, the seas, and the mountain rivers. Eight of them so far. They didn't arrive in a flash. They arrived in gigawatts.

This is their roster. You're on the next page.

TWO PATHS TO JOIN

FOR GRID-SCALE HEROES

You run a utility. You buy steel. You move terawatt-hours.

  • PPAs: long-term Power Purchase Agreements lock in green electrons at a fixed price. Your CFO sleeps.
  • Capacity auctions: bid renewables + storage into national markets and clear at a price the public funds.
  • Hydrogen valleys: co-locate electrolysers with offshore wind. Sell H₂ to industry next door.
  • Grid-flexibility markets: let demand-response, batteries, and pumped-hydro replace gas peakers.
  • Cross-border interconnectors: ENTSO-E's 10-year plan is the team's group chat.
FOR NEIGHBOURHOOD HEROES

You rent. You own. You charge a phone.

  • Rooftop solar: 5–10 kW kit on a south-facing roof. Pays back in ~6–10 years across most of Europe.
  • Balcony PV: two 400 W panels, plug into the wall. Legal in DE, AT, IT, FR, ES, BE, NL.
  • Energy co-ops: own a slice of a wind turbine for a few hundred euros. REScoop.eu lists 2,500+ across Europe.
  • Heat pumps: 3–4 kWh of warmth per kWh of electricity. The most powerful hero in your basement.
  • Demand-shifting: cheap-electricity hour apps (Tibber, Awattar). Run the dishwasher when wind blows.
